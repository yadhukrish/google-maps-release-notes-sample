# Google Maps Release Notes

This document summarizes feature releases, enhancements, and availability updates for the Google Maps mobile app (Android and iOS).

For platform API changes, see the [Google Maps Platform release notes](https://developers.google.com/maps/release-notes).

---

## March 2026

### Immersive Navigation

Google Maps now offers a redesigned driving experience called Immersive Navigation, described by Google as the biggest update to the navigation experience in over a decade.

**What's new:**

- **3D map view:** The driving map now renders a three-dimensional view of your surroundings, including buildings, overpasses, and terrain, generated from Street View and aerial imagery.
- **Road detail highlighting:** Lanes, crosswalks, traffic lights, and stop signs are highlighted contextually as you approach them, powered by Gemini's analysis of real-world imagery.
- **Broader route view:** The map now shows more of the upcoming route so you can anticipate lane changes and complex intersections before you reach them. Smart zoom activates automatically ahead of maneuvers that require attention.
- **Transparent buildings:** Structures that block the view of an upcoming turn are rendered semi-transparent so the road behind them remains visible.
- **Natural voice guidance:** Turn-by-turn instructions are rewritten to sound more conversational. Example: *"Go past this exit and take the next one for Illinois 43 South"* instead of *"In 0.4 miles, take Exit 43."*
- **Proactive alternate routes:** Maps now surfaces alternate route suggestions before you hit a delay, with a clear trade-off summary — for example, *"5 minutes longer, no toll."*
- **Destination preview:** Integrated Street View imagery is available at the destination to help you identify building entrances and parking options before you arrive.

**Availability:** Rolling out now on Android and iOS in the United States. Expansion to CarPlay, Android Auto, and vehicles with Google built-in is planned for later in 2026.

### Ask Maps

Ask Maps is a new Gemini-powered conversational feature built directly into the Google Maps app. Instead of searching by keyword or category, you can ask specific, real-world questions in natural language and receive a personalised response with a map view of the results.

**Example queries:**

- *"My phone is dying — where can I charge it without waiting in a long line for coffee?"*
- *"Is there a public tennis court with lights on that I can play at tonight?"*
- *"I'm headed to the Grand Canyon, Horseshoe Bend, and Coral Dunes. Any recommended stops along the way?"*

Ask Maps draws on community-contributed data, reviews, and place information to generate responses. Results are displayed as a customised map alongside the answer.

**To access Ask Maps:** Tap the **Ask Maps** button on the main map screen.

**Availability:** Rolling out now on Android and iOS in the United States and India.

## January 2026

### Gemini in Navigation — Walking and Cycling

Gemini hands-free assistance, previously available for driving, now extends to walking and cycling navigation.

**Walking:** During an active walking route, tap the Gemini icon or say *"Hey Google"* to ask contextual questions without leaving the navigation screen. Example queries include:
- *"What neighbourhood am I in?"*
- *"Are there cafes with a bathroom along my route?"*
- *"What are some must-see attractions nearby?"*

**Cycling:** Cyclists can access the same hands-free assistance without taking their hands off the handlebars. Supported queries include ETA checks, calendar lookups, and message dictation — for example, *"Text Sarah I'm 10 minutes behind."*

Gemini in Navigation supports follow-up questions, so you can refine a query mid-conversation without restarting. For example: ask for a budget-friendly restaurant with vegan options, then follow up with *"Does it have parking nearby?"*

**To activate:** Tap the Gemini icon in the top-right corner of the navigation screen, or use the *"Hey Google"* voice invocation.

**Availability:** Rolling out globally on iOS and Android wherever Gemini is available.

## November 2025

### Landmark-Based Navigation

Turn-by-turn directions now reference nearby landmarks — such as restaurants, petrol stations, or well-known buildings — in addition to street names and distances.

Example: *"Turn right after the Thai Siam Restaurant"* instead of *"Turn right in 200 metres."*

Landmarks are selected by Gemini, which cross-references over 250 million places in Google Maps with Street View imagery to identify landmarks that are clearly visible from the street and useful for navigation.

**Availability:** Rolling out on Android and iOS in the United States.

---

### Proactive Traffic Alerts

Google Maps now sends a notification when an unexpected delay — such as a road closure, accident, or heavy congestion — is detected on a route you travel regularly, even when you are not actively navigating.

The alert appears before your typical departure time and includes an estimated delay and a suggested alternate route.

**Availability:** Rolling out on Android and iOS. Works for saved home and work addresses and frequently travelled routes.

---

### Lens with Gemini

A new visual search feature is available in the Maps search bar. Point your camera at a restaurant, shop, or landmark to identify it and ask questions about it in conversation.

**To access:** Tap the camera icon in the Maps search bar.

**Availability:** Rolling out gradually in the United States on Android and iOS.
